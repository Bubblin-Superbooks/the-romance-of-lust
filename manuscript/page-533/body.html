“Quite right, my love; my dear Charles, you must endeavour to follow the wishes of your of aunt.”

Of course I promised, and with such a look of innocence that I could see they exchanged smiles at it. We sat down to luncheon. Afterwards the doctor, seating himself by my side, began a conversation on the historical subject I had been studying. Our conversation became really very interesting. The doctor was a man of great erudition, and of varied knowledge, and had a manner, special to himself, of making almost any subject most interesting. Hours flew by, and it was only when aunt entered about five o’clock, to take a cup of tea, as was her wont, that we were aware how time had flown.
